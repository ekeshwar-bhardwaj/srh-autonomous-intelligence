Strategy Context — RHG Autonomous Intelligence

Boardroom-grade synthesis of RHG's investment model, decision complexity, persona architecture, KPI hierarchy, AI agent stack, and demo narrative — designed as a single living reference for the Chairman, Investment Committee and CFO.

1Business Deconstruction

A. Investment Model

RHG operates as the investment arm of Waqf Sulaiman Alrajhi — one of the largest Islamic endowments globally — with ~SAR 40B AUM allocated across three structurally distinct pillars. Real Estate (39%) blends income-yielding assets with capital appreciation, currently mid-consolidation from 6 fragmented systems into a single Yardi instance. Financial Investments (35%) are deployed via 12 external fund managers across AMEA, APAC and Emerging Markets — the discipline is benchmark tracking and manager attribution, not direct trading. Direct Investments (26%) are 9 wholly-owned operating subsidiaries spanning manufacturing, poultry, agri, education, IT services and hospitality across KSA, Sudan and Egypt — the active workstream is lifecycle management and selective monetization.

B. Core Value Drivers

Returns are driven by four asymmetric levers: operating-subsidiary EBITDA growth (largest swing factor — Direct Investments contribute 41% of NAV growth), fund-manager alpha (9 of 12 mandates beat benchmark, 3 actively flagged for replacement), real estate yield + capital appreciation (rental yield 6.4%, REIDIN +5.8% YoY tailwind), and opportunistic divestment (3 subsidiaries flagged Divest-Ready, ~SAR 2.58B unlock potential). Macro sensitivities are concentrated in oil/Brent (Saudi sovereign budget proxy), SAIBOR / Saudi 10Y Sukuk (Sukuk re-pricing), Tadawul (35% of Financial Investments), and Sudan/Egypt FX (SAR 1.03B exposure across Atbara + Al Safa).

C. Decision Complexity

The Chairman and Investment Committee operate at the intersection of three competing horizons: an endowment perpetuity mandate (preserve real capital for the Waqf), annual distribution obligations (fund charitable causes — SAR 1.2B target FY26), and tactical capital deployment (deploy SAR 4.8B pipeline at attractive entry multiples). The result is a multi-objective optimisation problem where capital allocation, divestment timing, and risk concentration must be balanced simultaneously — exactly the workload AI is designed to compress from weeks to minutes.
